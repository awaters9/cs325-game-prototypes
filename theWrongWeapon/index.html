  • Prototype Goal: Dynamic box colliders that send projectile objects flying in different directions.
  • Player Experience Goals: I want players to feel stressed but also have a desire to get better. The player should want to increase their score as high as possible. When the player is skilled at the game they will feel a sense of pride. The reward is personal skill.
  • Gameplay: The player is allowed to move from side to side and jump. As well as throw out 2 directional inputs that determine which side they may swing their sword. Using the ARROW KEYS will let the player move and jump Using the A and D keys will determine which side the player wants to attack. The player is expected to prevent themself from getting hit by a bullet for as long as possible. All of the inputs given will assist them with this one goal. The bullets that the player must avoid will constantly bounce all over the screen and is expected to increase in their velocity every time it bounces. What isn't in the prototype is a score system and a proper GAME OVER screen.
  • Strategies: The best strategy is game awareness. This game exercises the players ability to keep watch of all the bullets moving around the screen at deciding when to deflect.
  • Story/Setting/Premise: There is no backstory and there aren't any character designs in mind. The gameworld is rather dark and bleak with simple objects and high constrast colors. The player plays a lil cube who's only goal in life is to survive for as long as possible and is skilled at deflecting bullets with it's tiny sword. The gameplay will grow in intensity the longer the game goes on. Eventually the player will lose.
  • Target Audience: People who enjoy simple arcade games with one key mechanic and also enjoy the times playing flash games on the internets from the 2000s.
